For best results on treadmill recordings, consider the following recommendations.

The key to quality data collection is **minimizing occlusions of body segments**. Every body segment should be visible by at least two cameras at all times. Adjust camera positions based on your treadmill configuration.

:::tip
Before finalizing your setup, have the participant perform the activity while you monitor each camera's field of view, and ensure the participant's body is visible on each device screen.
:::

## 1. Recording set up

The setups below are ranked in order of preference. Choose based on your treadmill configuration:

### a. Cameras facing the back of the participant (recommended)

Use this setup when the treadmill is placed against a wall or has a large front-facing display, and does not support a reverse mode (i.e., negative belt speed).

If using two cameras, start by positioning them at approximately **±30° from the participant's forward-facing direction**, then adjust as needed to reduce occlusions caused by guardrails.

If a third camera is available, placing it front-facing improves capture robustness. In that case, shift the two flanking cameras to **±45°**, while still being mindful of guardrail occlusions.

### b. Cameras facing the front of the participant

For treadmill without front guardrails, have the participant run **facing the cameras** can offer good results — this setup typically captures the full body with just two cameras.

If using two cameras, start by positioning them at approximately **±30° from the participant's forward-facing direction**, then adjust as needed to reduce occlusions caused by guardrails.

If a third camera is available, placing it front-facing improves capture robustness. In that case, shift the two flanking cameras to **±45°**, while still being mindful of guardrail occlusions.

## 2. Calibration
The checkerboard does not need to be aligned with the treadmill — simply ensure it is **fully visible to all cameras** and **perpendicular to the ground**.

## 3. Static pose
Have the participant face the direction they will be facing during the activity (e.g., back-facing if running back-facing the cameras). See set up configuration options below.
