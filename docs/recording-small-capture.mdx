Best practices for recording activities that require a small capture area, such as Countermovement Jump, Drop Vertical Jump or Squatting.

:::tip
Before finalizing your setup, have the participant perform the activity while you monitor each camera's field of view, and adjust camera orientation to ensure the full movement is visible from all cameras.
:::

## 1. Recording Setup

Ensure cameras are correctly positioned to capture movement spanning a large area.

### Set up the cameras

- Mount the 2 iPhones/iPads on tripods in **horizontal/landscape mode**.
  - *If using 3 phones, position the front-facing one in **vertical/portrait mode**.*
- Follow the setup diagram below for ideal tripod positions and field of view.
- Open the Model Health app on all phones and adjust positions until the **full capture area is visible** in every camera view.

## 2. Setup calibration

- Place the checkerboard **strictly perpendicular to the ground**, with its long edge parallel to the ground, visible by all phones, approximately **3-4 m from each phone**, .
- Once ready, press **"Calibrate"**.

Once calibration is done, the cherckerboard does not need to stay in view. 

## 3. Static pose

- Have your participant stand in static pose close to the position where you positioned the checkerboard, foot straight and arm slightly detached from the body.
