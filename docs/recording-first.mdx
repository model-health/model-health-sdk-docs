This guide walks you through your first complete 3D movement analysis with Model Health, from calibrating your set up to exploring your results.

## Key Concepts

Before you start, familiarise yourself with the core building blocks of the app:

- **Subject**: the patient, athlete, or person you are collecting data with.
- **Group**: a set of subjects grouped together for analysis.
- **Set up**: your iOS devices mounted on tripods and calibrated. Once calibrated, a set up can be reused for any activity with any subject. A minimum of 2 iOS devices is required.
- **Activity type**: the category of movement you are recording. We currently support 12 activity types (e.g., CMJ, Cutting manoeuvres, Hop test), including a "Custom activity" that captures 3D biomechanics of any movement.
- **Activity**: a single recording with a subject, e.g., a single Countermovement jump.
- **Metric**: a quantified indicator reflecting a specific characteristic of the subject's activity. Metrics are specific to each activity type.

## Step 1. Calibrate Your Set Up

Calibration tells the system exactly where your cameras are in space. You only need to do this once per physical set up . after that you can record as many activities as you want without recalibrating, as long as the devices haven't moved.

### 1.1. Start a session

In the web app, click **Record activity**, then **Set up session**.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page7_img1.png" alt="Record activity button" />

### 1.2. Choose your set up configuration

Select the configuration that matches the activities you plan to record. Each option shows which activity types it is recommended for.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page8_img1.png" alt="Choose setup configuration" />

### 1.3. Connect your devices

Install the Model Health app from the App Store on each of your iOS recording devices. Open the app and scan the on-screen QR code. This links the devices to your account so recordings are automatically associated with your session.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page9_img1.png" alt="Connect devices via QR code" />

### 1.4. Position your devices and checkerboard

Follow the on-screen calibration guide to position your devices and checkerboard:

- Mount all recording devices on tripods.
- Adjust the phones until the full capture area is visible in every screen.
- Place the checkerboard strictly perpendicular to the ground, with its long edge parallel to the ground and visible by all phones, approximately 3–5m from each phone.

Once everything is in position, hit **Calibrate setup**. The process takes about 30 seconds.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page10_img1.jpeg" alt="Position devices and calibrate" />

### 1.5. Calibration complete

When calibration succeeds, hit **I'm ready to record**. Your calibrated set up remains active for 12 hours . do not move the phones during this time.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page11_img1.png" alt="Calibration successful" />

## Step 2. Record an Activity

Recording an activity follows a 4-step flow: select subject → record static pose → select activity type → record activity.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page12_img5.png" alt="Recording flow overview" />

### 2.1. Select or create a subject

Choose an existing subject from the dropdown or create a new one, then hit **Continue**.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page13_img1.png" alt="Select subject" />

### 2.2. Record a static pose

Ask the subject to stand still in a neutral position (upright, feet parallel and pointing forward, arms slightly out, full body visible to all cameras). Hit **Record pose** . it takes about 3 seconds.

This step calibrates the biomechanical model to the subject's body dimensions.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page14_img1.png" alt="Record static pose" />

Once the pose is recorded, hit **Continue**.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page15_img1.png" alt="Static pose recorded" />

### 2.3. Select the activity type

Choose the activity you are about to record and hit **Confirm**.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page16_img1.png" alt="Select activity type" />

### 2.4. Start recording

Review the tips for best data quality, then hit **Start recording** when the subject is ready:

- Ensure the subject is fully visible to all cameras.
- Tight clothing that contrasts with the background improves tracking.
- Hit record 1 second before the subject begins the movement.

You can optionally add **tags** and **notes** before, during, or after the recording.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page17_img1.png" alt="Ready to record" />

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page18_img1.png" alt="Add tags and notes" />

### 2.5. Stop recording

Hit **Stop recording** as soon as the activity is complete. The shorter the video, the faster the processing.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page19_img1.png" alt="Stop recording" />

Processing starts automatically in the background. You can continue recording other activities or go directly to results.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page20_img1.png" alt="Processing started" />

## Step 3. Explore Results

### 3.1. View videos and 3D model

From the subject page, select the activity type, then select one or more recordings to review the synchronized video, 3D model, and kinematics data.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page22_img1.jpeg" alt="View results - replay" />

### 3.2. Visualize metrics

Switch to the **Metrics** tab to see key test metrics. Select multiple recordings to track changes over time.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page23_img1.png" alt="View results - metrics" />

### 3.3. Download reports

Use the download button (top right) to export single-activity or comparison PDF reports.

<ZoomableImage src="/img/docs/recording-first/page24_img1.png" alt="Download PDF report" />
