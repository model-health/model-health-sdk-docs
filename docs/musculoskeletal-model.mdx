:::note For researchers and clinicians
This page is a technical reference covering joint coordinate systems, degrees of freedom, and anatomical definitions.
:::

Our platform is built on [OpenSim](https://opensim.stanford.edu/), a widely used open-source software for musculoskeletal simulation that has supported thousands of researchers and professionals worldwide for over 15 years [[Delp et al. 2007](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18018689/), [Seth et al. 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30048444/)]. We use a musculoskeletal model from the OpenSim library that incorporates physiologically accurate joint definitions. This means that joints are constrained to move in ways that reflect real human anatomy—for example, the hip can rotate in three directions but cannot translate, just like a ball-and-socket joint in the human body. 

We use a scientifically-validated musculoskeletal model with 36 degrees of freedom [[Rajagopal 2016](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27392337/), [Lai et al. 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28900782/), [Uhlrich et al. 2022](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35798755/)].

Joints are constrained to move in anatomically realistic ways — for example, the hip can rotate in three directions but cannot translate, just like a ball-and-socket joint in the human body.

## Joint Definitions

### Ground–Pelvis

6 degrees of freedom — 3 translations and 3 rotations. Defines how the pelvis moves relative to the world frame (defined by the checkerboard).

**Rotations**

- **Pelvis tilt** — forward and backward tipping of the pelvis. Positive when the pelvis rotates backward.

  ![Pelvis tilt](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_list.png)

- **Pelvis list** — side-to-side tilting of the pelvis. Positive when the left part of the pelvis tilts upward.

  ![Pelvis list](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_tilt.png)

- **Pelvis rotation** — twisting of the pelvis around the vertical axis. Positive when the pelvis rotates to the left.

  ![Pelvis rotation](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_rotation.png)

:::note Biomechanics detail
Pelvis rotations follow ZXY (body-fixed) Euler angle convention: tilt → list → rotation.
:::

**Translations**

- **Pelvis tx** — forward/backward movement. Positive = pelvis translates forward.

  ![Pelvis tx](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_tx.png)

- **Pelvis ty** — up/down movement. Positive = pelvis translates upward.

  ![Pelvis ty](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_ty.png)

- **Pelvis tz** — side-to-side movement. Positive = pelvis translates to the right.

  ![Pelvis tz](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/pelvis_tz.png)

### Hip

3 degrees of freedom — 3 rotations. Defines how the femur moves relative to the pelvis.

- **Hip flexion-extension** — positive = flexion, negative = extension.

  ![Left hip flexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_flexion_l.png) ![Left hip extension](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_extension_l.png)

- **Hip adduction-abduction** — positive = adduction, negative = abduction.

  ![Left hip adduction](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_adduction_l.png) ![Left hip abduction](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_abduction_l.png)

- **Hip internal-external rotation** — positive = internal rotation, negative = external rotation.

  ![Left hip internal rotation](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_rotation_l.png) ![Left hip external rotation](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/hip_external_rotation_l.png)

:::note Biomechanics detail
Hip rotations follow ZXY (body-fixed) Euler angle convention: flexion → adduction → rotation.
:::

---

### Knee

1 degree of freedom. Defines how the tibia moves relative to the femur.

- **Knee flexion-extension** — positive = flexion, 0 = full extension.

  ![Left knee flexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/knee_flexion_l.png)

:::note Biomechanics detail
The knee isn't a simple hinge. While described by a single flexion-extension coordinate, it also involves small coupled rotations and translations. This means the lower leg shifts slightly inward during deep flexion.

  ![Knee neutral](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/knee_back.png) ![Deep knee flexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/knee_flexion_back_r.png)
:::

---

### Ankle

1 degree of freedom. Defines how the talus moves relative to the tibia.

- **Ankle dorsi-plantarflexion** — positive = dorsiflexion, negative = plantarflexion.

  ![Left ankle dorsiflexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/ankle_dorsi_l.png) ![Left ankle plantarflexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/ankle_plantar_l.png)

---

### Subtalar

1 degree of freedom. Defines how the calcaneus moves relative to the talus.

- **Foot supination-pronation** — positive = supination, negative = pronation.

  ![Left foot supination](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/subt_supination_l.png) ![Left foot pronation](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/subt_pronation_l.png)

---

### Lumbar

3 degrees of freedom — 3 rotations. Defines how the torso moves relative to the pelvis (at the L5 level).

- **Lumbar flexion-extension** — positive = extension, negative = flexion.

  ![Lumbar extension](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_extension.png) ![Lumbar flexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_flexion.png)

- **Lumbar bending** — positive = left shoulder moves upward, negative = right shoulder moves upward.

  ![Lumbar bending positive](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_bending_pos.png) ![Lumbar bending negative](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_bending_neg.png)

- **Lumbar rotation** — positive = torso rotates leftward, negative = torso rotates rightward.

  ![Lumbar rotation positive](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_rotation_pos.png) ![Lumbar rotation negative](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/lumbar_rotation_neg.png)

:::note Biomechanics detail
Lumbar rotations follow ZXY (body-fixed) Euler angle convention: flexion → bending → rotation.
:::

### Shoulder

6 degrees of freedom — 3 translations and 3 rotations. Models the full shoulder complex including the scapulothoracic segment.

**Translations** (scapulothoracic segment relative to torso)

- **Shoulder tx** — forward/backward. Positive = forward.

  ![Shoulder tx](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_tx.png)

- **Shoulder ty** — up/down. Positive = upward.

  ![Shoulder ty](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_ty.png)

- **Shoulder tz** — side-to-side. Positive = medially.

  ![Shoulder tz](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_tz.png)

**Rotations** (glenohumeral joint — humerus relative to scapulothoracic body)

- **Shoulder plane elevation** — angle of the plane in which the arm lifts. Positive = in front of torso, negative = behind torso.

  ![Plane elevation 50°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_plane_elev_50.png) ![Plane elevation 0°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_plane_elev_0.png) ![Plane elevation -50°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_plane_elev_-50.png)

- **Shoulder elevation** — how much the arm lifts away from the body. Positive = arm raising in any plane.

  ![Elevation 0°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_elev_0.png) ![Elevation 90°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_elev_90.png) ![Elevation 150°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_elev_150.png)

- **Shoulder axial rotation** — twisting of the upper arm around its own axis. Positive = internal rotation, negative = external rotation.

  ![Axial rotation 50°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_axial_rot_50.png) ![Axial rotation 0°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_axial_rot_0.png) ![Axial rotation -50°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/shoulder_axial_rot_-50.png)

:::note Biomechanics detail
Shoulder rotations follow YXY (body-fixed) Euler angle convention: plane elevation → elevation → axial rotation. This is recommended by the [International Society of Biomechanics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15844264/).
:::

---

### Elbow

2 degrees of freedom — 2 rotations. Defines ulna movement relative to humerus and radius movement relative to ulna.

- **Elbow flexion-extension** — 0 = full extension; increases with flexion.

  ![Left elbow flexion](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/elbow_flexion_l.png)

- **Forearm pronation-supination** — 0 = full supination (palm up); increases toward pronation (palm down).

  ![Forearm pronation 0°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/elbow_pronation_0_l.png) ![Forearm pronation 50°](/img/docs/musculoskeletal-model/elbow_pronation_50_l.png)
