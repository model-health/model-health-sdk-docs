## High-Level Best Practices

- Ensure you are connected to a stable internet connection. Video upload may fail with a poor connection.
- Do not move cameras once you've passed the calibration step. If you accidentally moved a camera, start a new session.
- The checkerboard is only used for calibrating the cameras — you can remove it after successfully completing the calibration step.

## Camera Setup

- Use tripods to hold the smartphone in a fixed position during the session.
- The rear-facing camera is used for recording — position the smartphones accordingly.
- Position the cameras to **avoid segment occlusions** during the task.
  - At least two cameras need to see every body segment at all times. Two cameras is sufficient for many tasks, but tasks where segments get occluded may benefit from an additional front-facing camera (e.g., single-leg activities).
  - For a two-camera setup, cameras at 30–45° off the forward-facing line is typically best. Pure sagittal views do not work well due to limb occlusion.
- **Avoid people in the background.** Our algorithms handle this, but it makes them slower and more error-prone.
- Do a short test with the participant performing the task and **check that the participant remains visible in each camera for the entire activity before calibrating**.
- Position the smartphone in **either landscape or portrait** orientation. If in landscape, make sure portrait orientation is **not locked** in your device settings — otherwise videos will be recorded in landscape format with a portrait orientation, leading to poor motion reconstruction.
- **Avoid having the participant exit and re-enter the capture volume.**

## Camera Calibration

**Environment:**
- Record in a well-lit environment.
- If recording outdoors with direct sun on the checkerboard, shade it before calibrating.

**Checkerboard dimensions:**
- Use a checkerboard on at least A4 paper with squares of at least 35mm. We recommend printing [this board](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/62468717bed6b421c89bbf36/6258dd3518244b061c1b02f5_Checkerboard_4x5_35mm.pdf).
- Ensure the checkerboard has a white border around all sides.
- Verify the dimensions of the squares after printing.
- **The number of columns and rows to enter in the web app is the number of black-to-black corners**, not the number of squares.

**Checkerboard position:**
- The checkerboard must be visible by all cameras.
- It should stand perpendicular to the ground, with its long edge parallel to the ground.
- Keep it within 5m of the cameras.
- It should not point straight at one of the cameras — move it up, down, left, or right to avoid this.
- Position it at the center of your capture volume, at least 2m from the cameras, while visible by all cameras.

▶️ [Watch: How to calibrate your cameras](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6TkFLEfQ0Y&feature=youtu.be)

:::tip New: 360° camera setup
You can now position cameras in a full 360° circle around the subject by calibrating them with the checkerboard **flat on the floor**. This is ideal for activities that benefit from both front and back views.
:::

## Data Collection

- Tight clothing that contrasts with a simple background and shoes that contrast with both the floor and pants, is helpful.
- Start recording at least 1 second before the participant begins the movement.
- For treadmill gait or fairly static movements, have the participant **punch one hand in the air above their shoulders** and bring it back down at some point during the trial. This facilitates camera synchronization.
- Colored dots next to trial names indicate processing status: **green** (success), **orange** (processing), **red** (error). Re-collect trials with a red dot when possible.
- Download data only when all dots have turned green (or red). Click red dots for details on what went wrong.
- To collect data on another participant without re-calibrating, press **"New session same setup"**.
