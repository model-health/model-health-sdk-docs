We offer a suite of pre-built modules for analyzing specific tasks. Each module segments the task (e.g., gait cycles for running or repetitions for squats) and computes key biomechanical parameters. Results can be visualized in a dashboard and are summarized in a PDF report alongside joint angles.

To access the advanced analysis modules, hit the **Analysis** button in the trial menu.

![Analysis button location](/img/docs/advanced-analysis/Doc2.png)

Select a module and hit **Run** — analyses typically complete in under two minutes.

:::tip
If an analysis fails immediately, try again — this may be a transient issue. If it fails after a while, the algorithm may have failed to segment the activity. **Make sure you run the analysis corresponding to the recorded task** — for example, running the Squats analysis on a jumping task will fail.
:::

## Joint Range of Motion

Computes the range of motion for each joint across the trial, reporting the minimum, maximum and amplitude.

## Overground Walking and Running

Identifies strides and computes spatiotemporal parameters for each stride. Averages are shown in the dashboard and PDF report. For running, thresholds for red/orange/green zones are based on normative data.

**Recommended setup:** participant walks or runs straight toward the cameras with no turns. Two cameras in landscape mode at ±30–40° from the walking direction works well. Position cameras so the participant enters and exits both fields of view at the same time. Three cameras with a front-facing one can reduce occlusion risk.

## Treadmill Walking and Running

Same outputs as overground. If possible, have the participant face the cameras while walking/running — this limits occlusion risk. Two cameras at ±30° works well. If the participant faces away from cameras, use three cameras including one sagittal view that sees the feet.

See [Activity-Specific Recommendations](/docs/activity-recommendations#treadmill-walking-and-running) for more detail.

## Squats

Works with double-leg and single-leg squats. Identifies repetitions and computes biomechanical parameters per repetition. Averages are in the dashboard and PDF report. Thresholds based on normative data.

## Drop Vertical Jump

Works with double-leg and single-leg drop vertical jumps.

:::warning
The participant should make a **single** drop vertical jump — not multiple.
:::

Identifies contact and toe-off times and computes biomechanical parameters. Recommend recording at **120 Hz**. For single-leg DVJ, three cameras (two at ±45° and one frontal) is recommended.

See [Activity-Specific Recommendations](/docs/activity-recommendations#drop-vertical-jump) for more detail.

## Countermovement Jump

Works with double-leg and single-leg CMJ, with one or more repetitions. Identifies events and computes biomechanical parameters. Recommend recording at **120 Hz**. For single-leg, three cameras is recommended.

## Sit-to-Stand

Works for any number of sit-to-stand repetitions. If five repetitions are identified, it also reports the time to complete five reps (a standard clinical measure). Thresholds based on normative data.

## Hop

Works for single, double and triple hops (including crossover hops). Identifies toe-off and contact events and calculates biomechanical parameters.

Recommend recording at **120 or 240 Hz** with three cameras — one frontal and two at ±45° — to reduce occlusion risk. With only two cameras, the non-contacting leg is often occluded.

▶️ [Watch: Hop analysis demo](https://youtu.be/-6R2TNndplU)

## 5-0-5

Works for 180-degree change-of-direction tasks (5-0-5 / 10-0-5 test). Identifies key events and calculates biomechanical parameters. Thresholds based on normative data.

Recommend recording at **120 or 240 Hz** with three cameras (one frontal and two at ±45°). Two sagittal cameras also works.

▶️ [Watch: 5-0-5 analysis demo](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Tz4HB27QQI)

See [Activity-Specific Recommendations](/docs/activity-recommendations#505-180-degree-change-of-direction) for more detail.

## Cut

Works for any change-of-direction tasks (e.g., 45- and 90-degree cuts). For 180-degree cuts, use the [5-0-5 analysis](#5-0-5) for the most relevant metrics. Identifies key events and calculates biomechanical parameters.

Recommend recording at **120 or 240 Hz** with three cameras (one frontal and two at ±45°). Two sagittal cameras also works.

See [Activity-Specific Recommendations](/docs/activity-recommendations#cut-any-change-of-direction) for more detail.
